Math at a glance

Math is the most theoritical subject. It is the toughest also as it is comprised of limited concepts but a variety of question can be made over those concepts.So without enough practice you won't be able to score good marks in it.

Math comprises of 5 parts :
Calculus, Algebra, Coordinate Geometry, Vector and 3D Geometry, Trigonometry.

CALCULUS

This topic is the most important topic in maths and you can expect 40% weightage given to this topic itself. Hence it is very important to focus a lot on this topic. Also some of the questions asked in JEE Advanced on this topic in the recent years are quite difficult and having ingenius solutions hence you should solve even the most difficult problems of this topic within the JEE syllabus.

The correct strategy of excelling in this topic is to keep in mind the various techniques/tricks used while solving questions and when you learn a new trick think that how it can be applied to other questions of similar type. Also you should solve a lot of problems in this topic to make it your strength and once you cannot solve a problem do look-up for its solution so that you can get to learn an important concept which was maybe missed while learning the concepts.

PROBLEMS ONLY
  • The best book and a must-buy for all the top JEE aspirants is Sameer Bansal.
    (Highly Recommended)

    This book is for those who feel confident in the techniques used for solving questions and now wish to solve questions of high difficulty level. The problems of this book are at the maximum level of difficulty JEE can ask. This book is most useful if you have its solutions also so that you can look for the solutions to the questions you couldn't solve.

BOTH CONCEPT AND PROBLEMS
  • The best books for this category is Amit M Agarwal's books on 'Differential Calculus' and 'Integral Calculus'.
    (Highly Recommended)
    If you feel that you need to strengthen your concepts before moving on to problems or if you feel that you need to solve problems in increasing level of difficulty then this is the best book. These books provide problems concept-wise in various difficulty levels hence it is best for beginners. There are sufficient number of solved problems too to guide you through the concept.

ALGEBRA

This is the most difficult topic of maths for most students and even top ranker's.This is due to the vast and different varieties of questions . Algebra comprises 35% of the JEE paper and usually is the section that examiner wishes to make difficult .Lots of practice is needed in this area to get a good rank . Refer to as many different books and Institute Material for this section .Read plenty of solved Examples as this will help you on how to approach the various questions.

Topics in Algebra :
Quadratic Equations, Logarithm, Binomial Theorem, Complex Number, Probability, Sequences And Series, Determinants, Matrices.

BOOKS

For Algebra ,one must practice a lot and that too from multiple sources. Solve your institute material and various books .Understand various approaches by reading plenty of solved examples.

  • A textbook for Algebra -SK Goyal (Highly Recommended)

    This book is highly relevant and according to the latest pattern and trends of questions asked by JEE .It illustrates most of the concepts of algebra with proper solved examples and unsolved excersises at the last .

  • Cengage Series for Algebra -(Highly Recommended)

    Once again Cengage series is one of the most suited books for JEE and for algebra it is not behind.Contains large number of questions but it slightly on the easier side as compared to the other 2 books .Contains proper theory and solved examples.

  • Higher Algebra by Hall and Knight - (Recommended)

    One of the Best books for Algebra read throughout the world .Very good for Sequences and Series and Binomial theorem .

COORDINATE GEOMETRY

This is one of the easiest and most scoring topic of JEE . Any mistake made in this area costs you a lot since your competitors will most likely ace this area .Speed and Accuracy are very important specially in this area .Most toppers do not lose marks in this area .

BOOKS
  • Skills in Mathematics-Coordinate Geometry (Highly Recommended)

    This is the best book for coordinate Geometry and must be solved completely . Parabola,Ellipse,Hyperbola in this book are highly relevant for JEE .Read all the Solved problems in the book .They will help you build approaches on high to solve a problem especially of locus .

  • Cengage Series for Coordinate Geometry-(Highly Recommended)

    Contains detailed theory along with JEE relevant questions

  • SL Loney -(Recommended)

    One of the oldest books for Coordinate Geometry . Contains proper theory and also various formulas and some non-trivial results which may not be known to you but can be used directly in problems .

VECTOR AND 3D GEOMETRY

Vector and 3D is an essential part of JEE and there are usually about 3-4 questions from this topic every year in JEE Advanced .Most of the questions in vector are repeated ,thus solve archive fully and specially the subjective questions .For 3D most of the questions are cake walk if you have a good understanding of vector products and also their geometrical significance. It is strongly recommended to solve the questions of 3D by the vector method since it often proves to be the shorter method and in competitive exams, saving time is an essential factor.

BOOKS
  • Skills in Mathematics -Vector and 3D Geometry -(Highly Recommended)

    The book has covered most of the concepts and has good geometrical problems of vectors along with good approaches. Highly recommended to solve the same .

  • G Tewani -Cengage Series (Highly Recommemded)

    The book contains good solved problems covering most of the concepts and has ample of problems for the student to practice .

TRIGONOMETRY

Trigonometry is a quite vast topic in itself. It is one of those topics in which difficult questions can be made easily. There are a lot of trigonometry problems that have solutions through complex numbers, polynomials etc. and hence can be very difficult to deal with. But for JEE Advanced purpose only, there is quite less chance of such questions appearing in the exam since JEE usually asks simple questions from Trigonometry most of which are formula based.

BOOKS
  • The best book for Trigonometry is of Amit M Agarwal. It first illustrates the concept and then poses problems on that concept. This book is designed for beginners who want to excel in the topic. It has a lot of solved examples to guide you through the various techniques of manipulation of terms used frequently in trigonometry. If you feel weak in this topic then you should go for it, I am sure you would benefit a lot from it.
    (Highly recommended)

GENERAL MATHS BOOKS

  • Vikas Gupta and Pankaj Joshi is the best book for revision purposes.
    (Highly recommended)

    This book is best for revision purpose, The most important part of JEE preparation is revision of the concepts you have already learnt. For this purpose you need a book which has around 50 good questions from each chapter and the problems should cover almost all the important concepts. Hence this book is ideal for this purpose.

  • A Das Gupta is an old Classic book. It mainly has problems from each chapter of mathematics some of which have been excluded from the current syllabus. Many of the problems are solved illustrating the ideas used to solve problems. It also contains chapter-wise tests so that you know your level of preparation in that chapter.
    (Recommended)

ARCHIVE

  • Archive/Past Year papers are a must for every JEE aspirant. It gives you an idea about the type of questions asked in the exam and also the difficulty level of the problems. It also gives you an idea about whether your preparation is sufficient or not. I would recommend you to solve this book chapter-wise when you feel that your preparation of that chapter is sufficient to crack JEE problems or not. This book is for both JEE Mains and JEE Advanced and it is with solutions too.
    (Highly recommended)
